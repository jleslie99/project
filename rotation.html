2021 Chicago White Sox

Rotation

The starting rotation consists of the starting pitchers on the roster. This is usually a rotation of 5 pitchers. Each pitcher starts one game and then will rest for the next four. This is because they throw close to 100 pitches in those outings, so they require a lot of rest.

Lucas Giolito, Starting Pitcher

Throws: R, Hits: R

Lucas Giolito was one of the worst pitchers in baseball in 2018, but changed a lot of his mechanics and improved drastically in 2020. He continued this improvement in 2021 and cemented himself as the ace of the Sox pitching staff

Lance Lynn, Starting Pitcher

Throws: R, Hits: R

Lance Lynn was acquired via trade in the most recent offseason. He was one of the top pitchers in the league in 2020 and is a great addition to the rotation.

Dallas Keuchel, Starting Pitcher

Throws: L, Hits: L

Dallas Keuchel signed as a free agent before the 2020 season. He is an experienced veteran who doesn't get a lot of strikeouts, but has been consistent throughout his career.

Dylan Cease, Starting Pitcher

Throws: R, Hits: R

Dylan Cease was aquired from the Chicago Cubs as a prospect. His results have been mixed in his brief career that starting in 2019, but has made changes going into 2021. He is hopeful to build on his potential this year.

Carlos Rodon, Starting Pitcher

Throws: L, Hits: L

Rodon has been with the team for around 5 years now, but has struggled with injuries throughout his career. When healthy he is a very solid left-handed starter, so Sox fans are hopeful he can contribute for the whole season in 2021.
